About Me

Currently at NYU's Riviere Robot Lab, I own the full electromechanical design of a free-flyer space robot: from propeller placement and center-of-mass alignment to component selection, schematic design, and industrial-grade safety systems. I'm one of the pilots generating training data for its autonomous control algorithms — so I understand both what the hardware builds and how it behaves when autonomy takes over.

Before grad school I designed and fabricated the complete ECU wiring harness and battery pack for an electric race vehicle at Formula Student, leading the team to 1st place at FMAE. I worked on EV wiring architecture at TATA Motors, drone autonomy pipelines at Flytbase, and built a drone-based inspection roadmap for the Airport Authority of India. Earlier this year I won the MIT Hardware Hackathon for building an AI-powered smart glasses system that trains hardware teams in real time.

Open to hardware engineering, electrical engineering, electromechanical systems, and field/deployment engineering roles globally.
